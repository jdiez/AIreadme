# Strategyzer Methodology: Framework and Resources

## Overview of Strategyzer Methodology

### Foundation and Philosophy

**Strategyzer** was founded by **Alexander Osterwalder** and **Yves Pigneur**, building on their groundbreaking work in business model innovation that began with the creation of the Business Model Canvas. The methodology is based on the principle that innovation success requires systematic approaches to understanding customers, designing value propositions, and testing business model assumptions before making significant investments.

The Strategyzer approach emphasizes **evidence-based innovation** through structured experimentation, moving organizations away from opinion-based decision making toward data-driven validation of critical business assumptions.

### Core Methodology Components

#### Business Model Canvas

**Purpose**: Provides a visual framework for describing, designing, challenging, and pivoting business models.

**Nine Building Blocks**:
- **Value Propositions**: The bundle of products and services that create value for specific customer segments
- **Customer Segments**: Different groups of people or organizations an enterprise aims to reach and serve
- **Channels**: How a company communicates with and reaches its customer segments to deliver value
- **Customer Relationships**: Types of relationships a company establishes with specific customer segments
- **Revenue Streams**: Cash a company generates from each customer segment
- **Key Resources**: Most important assets required to make a business model work
- **Key Activities**: Most important actions a company must take to operate successfully
- **Key Partnerships**: Network of suppliers and partners that make the business model work
- **Cost Structure**: All costs incurred to operate a business model

**ODSAI Application**: For AI innovation projects, the Business Model Canvas helps map how data science solutions create value for different healthcare stakeholders, from clinicians and patients to payers and pharmaceutical partners.

#### Value Proposition Canvas

**Purpose**: Achieves fit between what you make and what customers want through systematic analysis of customer needs and corresponding value creation.

**Customer Profile Components**:
- **Customer Jobs**: Tasks customers are trying to perform, problems they're solving, or needs they're satisfying
- **Pains**: Bad outcomes, risks, and obstacles related to customer jobs
- **Gains**: Outcomes and benefits customers want to achieve

**Value Map Components**:
- **Products & Services**: List of what you offer to help customers get jobs done
- **Pain Relievers**: How your products and services alleviate customer pains
- **Gain Creators**: How your products and services create customer gains

**ODSAI Application**: Essential for understanding complex healthcare stakeholder needs and ensuring AI solutions address real clinical workflows, patient outcomes, and operational challenges.

#### Testing and Validation Framework

**Testing Cards Methodology**: Systematic approach to designing experiments that test critical business model assumptions with minimal time and resource investment.

**Testing Categories**:
- **Desirability Testing**: Do customers want this solution?
- **Feasibility Testing**: Can we build and deliver this solution?
- **Viability Testing**: Should we build this solution (business case)?

**Evidence Progression**: Framework for moving from weak evidence (opinions, assumptions) to strong evidence (validated customer behavior and measurable outcomes).

**ODSAI Application**: Particularly valuable for healthcare AI where regulatory requirements, clinical validation, and stakeholder adoption create complex validation requirements.
