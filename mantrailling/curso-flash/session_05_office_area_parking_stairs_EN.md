# Session 05 — Office Area and Underground Parking Stairs (Kocher Method)

## General Information

**Level:** Advanced

**Method:** Kevin Kocher (Positive Trailing)

**Estimated duration:** 35-45 minutes per team

**Main objective:** The dog follows a trail through an office district with two turns (left + right) and must locate the figurant hiding in the **stairwell of an underground car park** — a descent of approximately 15 steps below street level.

---

## Skills Trained

1. **Trailing in an office environment**: Wide pavements, tall buildings, smooth surfaces — scent behaves differently alongside glass and concrete facades
2. **Double turn in quick succession**: Left followed by right — the dog must resolve two direction changes in a short distance
3. **Level-change detection**: The trail descends below street level — the dog must detect that the scent goes down the stairs
4. **Stairwell / underground access marking**: The dog indicates the figurant descended — the handler must interpret this correctly
5. **Confined space work**: Parking stairs are narrow, dark, and echoey — a multi-sensory challenge

### Phase 1: Main Street — Segment 1 (3-5 minutes)

- Present scent article (5-15 seconds)
- Verbal cue: "Search" (once only)
- Dog follows trail along Main Street pavement
- Possible sniffing of glass facades (scent reflection)
- Resolves Turn 1 (left)

### Phase 2: Side Streets — Segments 2-3 (3-5 minutes)

- Dog works Side Street A (25m)
- Resolves Turn 2 (right)
- Works Side Street B (15m)
- Approaches parking entrance

### Phase 3: Parking Stairs — Critical Moment (2-5 minutes)

- Dog detects intense scent cone at stairwell mouth
- May hesitate, peer in, mark the entrance
- **HANDLER: Accompanies without forcing. Loose line. Dog's pace.**
- If dog will not descend: instructor evaluates options
- Dog descends stairs (handler accompanies)
- Locates figurant at foot of stairs

### Phase 4: Find and Celebration (1-2 minutes)

- **EXPLOSIVE celebration** from figurant
- Calibrate volume: echo in stairwell amplifies sounds
- Play + food (30-60 seconds)
- Climb stairs together in positive atmosphere

## Kocher Method Notes

- **Stairs are the central challenge**: Not all dogs are accustomed to descending unfamiliar stairs. If the dog has never gone down similar stairs, conduct 1-2 familiarisation sessions with stairs BEFORE this exercise (no trailing, just positive walkthrough with treats on every step).
- **Echo can startle**: Parking stairwells have particular acoustics. If the dog reacts to the echo of its own footsteps or to the amplified celebration sound, calibrate the celebration intensity.
- **The olfactory funnel is an advantage**: Scent concentrates in the stairwell shaft. The dog will detect a very intense cone — this makes the decision to descend easier. Use this to your advantage.
- **Do not force the descent**: If the dog will not go down, marking the entrance (sitting and looking at the stairs) is a VALID result. The figurant can come up. The objective is still met.
- **Loose line on stairs**: The handler MUST maintain a loose line even on the stairs. The temptation to "secure" the dog with tension is strong — resist it. The dog needs freedom of movement on the steps.
- **Positive ascent**: After the celebration, climb the stairs together in a relaxed, positive atmosphere. The dog should associate the entire location (down + up) with an excellent experience.
- **Proportional celebration**: This exercise has three difficulties (double turn + level change + confined space). The reward must be exceptional.
