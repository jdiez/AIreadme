# Session 02 — Fence Door Mark & Concealed Figurant (Kocher Method)

## General Information

**Level:** Intermediate-Advanced

**Method:** Kevin Kocher (Positive Trailing)

**Estimated duration:** 30-40 minutes per team

**Main objective:** The dog must (1) **mark a closed door** through which the figurant passed, and (2) locate a **figurant lying on the ground covered with a camouflage blanket** in a green area with trees and bushes.

---

## Skills Trained

1. **Obstacle marking**: The dog indicates to the handler that the trail continues through a barrier it cannot cross
2. **Ground-level find**: Figurant lying down and camouflaged — the dog cannot rely on sight
3. **Terrain transition**: Parking (asphalt) → green area (grass, earth, vegetation)

### Phase 1: Dog Works — Parking (3 minutes)

- Present scent article (5-15 seconds)
- Verbal cue: "Search"
- Dog follows the trail between parked cars
- Advances toward the fence

### Phase 2: Door Mark — Key Moment (2-5 minutes)

- Dog reaches the closed door
- Trail continues on the other side — dog detects this
- **Dog MARKS the door** (trained alert)
- Handler acknowledges the mark → opens the door
- Dog continues

### Phase 3: Green Area — Final Find (5 minutes)

- Dog enters the green area
- Terrain change: asphalt → grass/earth
- Scent turbulence from vegetation — dog works slower
- Locates the figurant lying under the blanket
- **Alerts** on the concealed figurant

### Phase 4: Reward (1 minute)

- Figurant reveals themselves → **maximum celebration**
- Play + food for 30-60 seconds
- Dog associates: marking + concealed find = total success

## Expected Behavior Table

| Point | Expected Behavior | If Not |
|---|---|---|
| Parking | Follows trail between cars | Distractions → clear area of other people/dogs |
| Closed door | Sniffs intensely, tries to pass | Normal — wait |
| Closed door | **Marks the door** (trained alert) | No mark → train door marking as isolated exercise first |
| Handler opens door | Dog resumes trail immediately | Loses interest → figurant makes soft noise from hiding spot |
| Green area | Nose down, searching in vegetation | Head up (visual search) → reduce distance to 25m |
| Camo blanket | Alerts on concealed figurant | Walks past → figurant makes minimal noise, reduce camo next time |

## Progression

| Session | Door-to-hide distance | Camouflage | Added Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|
| First time | 25m | Partial blanket (feet visible) | None |
| Repetition 2 | 50m | Full blanket (this session) | Standard |
| Repetition 3 | 75m | Blanket + leaves/branches on top | Dense vegetation |
| Repetition 4 | 100m | Full camo + crosswind | Operational conditions |

---

## Kocher Method Notes

- **Door MUST be closed**: If left open, the dog passes through and doesn't learn to mark. No learning without the obstacle.
- **Marking is trained separately first**: If the dog has never marked a door, run specific marking sessions BEFORE combining with a full trail.
- **Neutral-scent blanket**: The camo blanket must be washed and aired. The only detectable scent should be from the figurant underneath.
- **No corrections**: If the dog doesn't mark, it is NOT forced. Reduce difficulty and retry.
- **HUGE celebration at the end**: This exercise has two challenges (door + camouflage). The reward must be proportional to the effort.
