# Session 01 — Dual Figurant Split (Kocher Method)

## General Information

**Level:** Intermediate

**Method:** Kevin Kocher (Positive Trailing)

**Estimated duration:** 30-40 minutes per team

**Main objective:** Train the dog's scent discrimination when two people walk together and split in opposite directions. The dog must follow ONLY the figurant whose scent matches the scent article.

---

## Exercise Fundamentals

### Why this exercise?

1. **Discrimination in motion**: Two people walk together — the dog must separate the trails at the split
2. **Decision making**: At the split point, the dog chooses a direction based ONLY on scent
3. **Human distractor**: Figurant B acts as a negative distractor — same route, different scent
4. **Visual barrier**: Cars conceal both figurants — the dog cannot rely on sight
5. **Wind factor**: Strong wind at the split zone disperses and mixes scent cones — real olfactory challenge

---

## Required Materials

- **Working harness** (different from walking harness)
- **Long line** of 7-10 meters
- **Scent article** from Figurant A in ziplock bag
- **HIGH VALUE reward** (carried by Figurant A)
- **2 figurants** (ideally unknown to the dog)
- **Logbook**
- **Water** for the dog

---

## Environmental Conditions — Wind

**CRITICAL FACTOR: Strong wind at the split zone.**

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Intensity** | Strong — significantly disperses scent cones |
| **Affected zone** | Primarily the split point and open street |
| **Effect in corridor** | Minor (walls partially protect) |
| **Effect at split** | Maximum — scent dragged, mixed, diluted |
| **Consequence** | The dog will need more time and more checks at the split |

## Procedure

### Phase 0: Preparation (5 minutes)

```
INSTRUCTOR BRIEFING:
│
├─ Both figurants must have EQUAL energy with the dog
├─ The scent article is ONLY from Figurant A
├─ The reward is carried ONLY by Figurant A
├─ Figurant B must NOT have a reward or celebrate
└─ If the dog goes to Figurant B, they IGNORE it completely
```

### Phase 1: Motivation at Start Point — Next to Warehouse (3 minutes)

1. Both figurants stand with the dog at the start point, **next to the industrial warehouse** (on the right).
2. **Both** motivate the dog: play, voice, movement.
3. **Figurant A** places the scent article (personal clothing) on the ground.
4. The dog is taken away with the handler out of sight.

```

       └─ Fig.A leaves item ──┘

```

### Phase 2: Trail Laying (5 minutes)

5. Both figurants walk 20m from the warehouse to the corridor corner.
6. They turn **left** and enter the narrow corridor (50m), walking **together, side by side**.
7. At the end of the corridor, they reach the open street **(strong wind zone)**.
8. **Figurant A** turns **RIGHT**, walks 20m, hides behind a car.
9. **Figurant B** turns **LEFT**, walks 20m, hides behind a different car.

```

FIGURANT A + FIGURANT B                     HANDLER + DOG
    │                                             │
    ├─ Walk together through corridor             ├─ Out of sight
    │  (50m, normal pace)                          │
    │                                             │
    ├─ Reach end of corridor                      │

    │                                             │

    │                                             │
    └─ Both in position                           ├─ Instructor gives
                                                  │  "ready" signal
                                                  │
                                                  └─ Approach start
                                                     point
```

### Phase 3: Dog Works

9. Handler presents scent article to the dog (5-15 seconds).
10. Handler gives soft verbal cue ("Search" / "Find").
11. Dog follows the trail through the corridor.

### Phase 4: The Split — Key Moment

12. At the end of the corridor, the dog reaches the **split point**.
13. It is **normal** for the dog to hesitate — there is scent in both directions.
14. The handler **does NOT intervene**. Loose line. Let the dog decide.
15. The dog must **commit** to Figurant A's direction (right).

```

                      │

                      ├─ Sniffs right... 👃
```

### Phase 5: Find and Reward

16. Dog locates Figurant A behind the car.
17. Dog alerts (trained indication).
18. **Figurant A celebrates with maximum enthusiasm** — play + food (30-60 sec).

## Kocher Method Notes

- **No corrections**: If the dog chooses wrong, it is NOT corrected. Ignore the error and retry.
- **Both figurants must have IDENTICAL energy** at initial motivation — the only differentiator is scent.
- **The corridor concentrates scent**: Both trails mix in a narrow space, making the split the real test.
- **Never punish hesitation**: Hesitation at the split means the dog is processing. It's a good sign.
- **Figurant B is passive**: If the dog finds them, Figurant B does not react. No eye contact, no talking, no movement.
- **The industrial warehouse** provides a visual reference and partial wind protection at the start. The dog begins in a relatively sheltered zone.
- **Wind factor — CRITICAL in this session**: Strong wind at the split zone is a deliberate difficulty element. It drags and mixes scent cones. The handler MUST give the dog more time (double or triple the usual). Extended hesitation is NOT an error — the dog is processing degraded information.
- **Ground scent vs. airborne scent**: With strong wind, airborne scent disperses quickly. Residual ground scent is more stable. If the dog drops its nose to the ground at the split, that's a good sign — it's seeking more reliable scent.
- **If wind is excessive**: Consider reducing separation distance (10m instead of 20m) or repeating the session on a calmer day to consolidate before adding the wind factor.
