# Session 04 — Backtrack in Urban Grid (Kocher Method)

## General Information

**Level:** Intermediate-Advanced

**Method:** Kevin Kocher (Positive Trailing)

**Estimated duration:** 35-45 minutes per team

**Main objective:** The dog follows a trail with a **backtrack** in a neighbourhood with a grid pattern. The figurant walks along Main Street, passes Street A, continues toward Street B, backtracks to Street A (overlap zone between Street A and Street B), turns onto Street A, takes Street C (which connects Street A and Street B), and turns again onto Street B where they hide — forcing the dog to resolve the double-scent zone and three consecutive turns.

---

## Skills Trained

1. **Direction discrimination in backtrack**: The dog must determine which direction the trail was walked when outbound and return footsteps overlap between Street A and Street B
2. **Overlap zone management**: The section of Main Street between Street A and Street B has a double layer of scent — the dog must resolve the ambiguity
3. **Decision making at multiple intersections**: The dog must resolve three consecutive turns (Street A, Street C, Street B)
4. **Urban grid work**: Perpendicular streets with multiple directional options
5. **Handler trust during confusion**: The handler will see the dog hesitate in the overlap zone — must wait without intervening

### Phase 1: Segments 1-2 — Main Street (3-5 minutes)

- Present scent article (5-15 seconds)
- Verbal cue: "Search" (once only)
- Dog follows trail along Main Street
- Passes Intersection 1 (Street A) without stopping (scent goes straight)
- Continues 15m to turnaround point (between Street A and Street B)

### Phase 2: Turnaround Point — Critical Moment (2-5 minutes)

- Dog detects that trail "ends" and returns
- May circle, sniff intensely, pause
- **HANDLER: Does not intervene. Loose line. Silence.**
- Dog resolves and backtracks following the return scent

### Phase 3: Intersection 1 / Street A — Decision 1 (2-5 minutes)

- Dog arrives at intersection for second time
- Detects figurant's left turn onto Street A
- May briefly explore before deciding
- Turns left and follows along Street A
- **HANDLER: Maximum patience. Do not indicate direction.**

### Phase 4: Street C — Decision 2 (1-3 minutes)

- Clean scent on Street A, dog regains pace
- Reaches intersection with Street C
- Detects right turn
- Turns right onto Street C (connects Street A and Street B, 30m)
- **HANDLER: Loose line. Trust the dog.**

### Phase 5: Street B — Decision 3 (1-3 minutes)

- Dog follows Street C up to Street B
- Detects figurant's turn onto Street B
- Turns and follows along Street B
- **HANDLER: Loose line.**

### Phase 6: Street B and Find (2-3 minutes)

- Final stretch, clean scent — dog accelerates
- Locates figurant in doorway
- **EXPLOSIVE celebration** from figurant
- Play + food (30-60 seconds)
- Handler joins final celebration

## Kocher Method Notes

- **The backtrack is the central challenge**: The overlap zone between Street A and Street B has two layers of scent from the same figurant. The dog must discriminate by scent FRESHNESS (the return scent is more recent). This is an advanced olfactory skill.
- **Do not intervene in the overlap zone**: The dog NEEDS time to process the double layer. Circles, intense sniffing, and pauses are signs it is working, not that it is lost.
- **Three turns after the backtrack**: After resolving the overlap, the dog must solve three consecutive turns (Street A, Street C, Street B). Each turn is an independent decision-making test.
- **Street C as connector**: Street C connects Street A and Street B (one block, ~30m). This long stretch without turns allows the dog to regain confidence between decisions.
- **The intersection is the second test**: When the dog returns to the intersection it already passed, it has new information. It must choose correctly. If it chooses wrong, it will realise through scent loss — self-correction is part of the learning.
- **Loose line is CRITICAL here**: The handler will see the dog "go backwards" and feel the urge to correct. DO NOT. The dog is following the trail correctly.
- **Guaranteed success**: If the dog cannot resolve the backtrack within reasonable attempts, the instructor may ask the figurant to move closer to the intersection to shorten the exercise. Always end on a positive note.
- **Proportional celebration**: This exercise has multiple difficulties (backtrack + overlap + three grid turns). The reward must be exceptional.
